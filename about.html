About

Bacolod City, often referred to as the 'City of Smiles,' is a vibrant destination in the Philippines known for its warm hospitality, rich cultural heritage, and delectable cuisine. Located in the heart of Negros Occidental, Bacolod offers a unique blend of historical sites, modern attractions, and a lively atmosphere, making it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a taste of authentic Filipino charm.

The story of Bacolod City

History of Bacolod City

Bacolod is a city on the northwest coast of Negros Island in the Philippines. The 19th-century San Sebastian Cathedral stands beside Bacolod Public Plaza. Its original bells are on display in the churchyard outside.

The Dizon-Ramos Museum is a 1950s house packed with memorabilia and photographs of the prominent family that once lived there. The Capitol Park and Lagoon has a lake and 2 golden water buffalo statues.
